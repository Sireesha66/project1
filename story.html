Virat Kohli

Virat Kohli, born on November 5, 1988, is an Indian cricketer and former captain of the Indian national cricket team. He is considered one of the greatest batsmen in the world and has numerous records to his name.

Kohli made his international debut in 2008 and quickly rose through the ranks with his exceptional batting skills. Known for his aggressive playing style and incredible consistency, he has shattered many batting records. He holds the record for the fastest century in One Day Internationals (ODIs) and has the most centuries in ODIs while chasing.

Kohli's passion for the game is evident in his performances. He has been awarded the prestigious Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy for ICC Cricketer of the Year multiple times and has been ranked as the number one batsman in both Test and ODI formats by the ICC.

Apart from his cricketing achievements, Kohli is also known for his philanthropic work. He actively supports various causes, including child welfare and education.

Virat Kohli's dedication, talent, and sportsmanship have made him an inspiration for aspiring cricketers around the world. His love for the game and his relentless pursuit of excellence have earned him a special place in the hearts of cricket fans globally.

There’s a very good reason Virat Kohli is referred to as the ‘King’. The Indian captain’s batting record is hardly matched by any other cricketer on the planet, with his aggressive approach to the game also making him a player fans flock to watch.

Kohli always dreamed of representing his country in cricket and had his first opportunity as a teenager when he played for the India under-19 team in 2006, guiding the same team to a World Cup win in 2008
